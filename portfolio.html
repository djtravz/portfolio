Intros:

Every morning at Wilson High School, the Wilson Media Productions club runs the Morning Announcements. My freshman year, the interns started to make intros to the announcements that were based on TV show intros. Ever since then, there has been a constant flow of intros coming from different people from around the club, here are mine.

Modern Family

This was the first intro I made and my first major foray into the media club as a whole. One of the interns at the time recorded all the clips with the teachers. They even used my empty picture frame because the club didn't even have one (I forgot to take it home for a few months)

Law and Order

We needed an intro to start off the school year, so we came in a few weeks before school started and decided to do an intro based on Law and Order. We got the script for the intro ready and recorded that day, then went out and recorded a lot of random footage throughout the school. We then split into groups to edit the final thing and I worked alone. We submitted all the final renders and voted on which one we liked the best (it wasn't mine). The thing I was most proud of at the time was the transitions with the title graphic because it very closely matches the original.

Giraffes Fighting

This was made right before the primary elections that year on a whim the day before because we found the original clip very funny. Once we showed it, we were informed that it is actually very harmful to the giraffes because their necks sometimes break, so it was quite insensitive to show as a joke.

Super Smash Bros.

I worked on this one for the launch of Super Smash Bros. Ultimate. I got all the people in the club to submit videos of them doing something funny and I made a template to use. It's based off the character reveal trailers specifically for Smash 4 because it looks a lot more interesting than the new game. Most other memes using this format will either have the wrong font, incorrectly positioned, or wrong layering which bothers me after seeing myself do it perfectly.

Wii Startup

This intro is based off of the startup on the Wii. It uses old intros that I could compile as the fillers for the “channels” of the wii. There are so many layers inside of this menu that you really don't notice when using it. The cursor is all manually keyframed and every channel it hovers over gets highlighted with the same animation. I also replaced all the instances of “Wii” with “WMP” which looks really really good. Even the time is updated to the time that we normally started announcements. My favorite part is definitely the 3 seconds at the beginning because it took me well over an hour to get everything perfect.

Cowboy Bebop

This is the biggest intro I had worked on to this point. I started it and got a lot of the basics done on a 7 day adobe trial because the school canceled my subscription over the summer. I had a friend help me do the animations which took them about a month. I had to crop the song because it would be past our max length for intros if I didn't. I have gotten so used to it that the original sounds wrong. The thing that I like the least about it is the text flying in the background. It really should have just been random Japanese characters because it's too easy to read (or maybe even translating what it originally says), while the ending section is definitely my favorite. The ending point was profile pictures for all of the officers of the Media club at the time, so I got the outlines and made them like the original show. I also enjoy the amount of effort I put into the first 3 seconds. Each of those 4 sections is only like 5 frames, but it looks really good, especially the last two. Something else interesting is that I had to make the specks on the logo in the end just in Microsoft Paint because it had simple editing which was exactly what I needed.

Ducktales Moon Theme

I made this one while waiting to get the animations back from the bebop intro so I managed to do it in a single night. I got the planet spinning from a pixel art planet generator app which was pretty fun to work with. I think it turned out really well for how little effort actually put into it.

Wilson5

This tops Bebop as the biggest intro at the time. We decided in September that we needed to start working on an intro for Halloween so we were going through spooky ideas and watched all of the Local58 videos on YouTube. I was instantly motivated and a friend designed the basis for what I would base the logo off of. I made it a lot better in Photoshop and am very happy with how it turned out. I originally started by just making a simple intro. I then started working on the actual scary section and it turned out super badly at first because I was trying to make my own thing. After a lot of revisions we realized that it was just slowly turning into a direct copy of Weather Service, but that's alright. I recorded the footage needed and got it done relatively quickly. The plan was to run the normal looking retro intro for the whole month then transform it into the scary one directly on October 31st, but the principal vetoed it the week before, which was disappointing, but valid since we don't want to cause panic. We ended up just sending it out in an email. A few months later there was a FilmFest going on in the area, so I made some minor fixes to it and extended it to the 4:30 mark that was required. When I went to submit it, there were some super weird issues that caused me not to be able to, but I don't really mind that much. (this is not the version that I have uploaded here)

Metal Gear Solid

I saw the death screen for Metal Gear Solid and instantly thought of how cool it would look as an intro so I started working on it. The entire thing is made pretty badly with every line being its own video just slotted into place, but it turned out well enough, in my opinion. We even turned it into the design for the next year's club shirt.

Game & Watch

I had started this project with high hopes, but after working on it for a while, I realized that it wasn't actually going to turn out very well. It was cool in concept and production, but wasn't actually good in practice.

Frozen

This was our winter intro for this year. I used Cinema4D, a 3D rendering program, to create it, which I had no experience with going into. I managed to make something pretty alright, but it definitely could be better in terms of the cover up of the original ice and the tracking of the new ice onto the background. Also the text is super wide so it is not too much like the original, but its still alright. There are 30 seconds of stillness at the end because we used the first last bit of the music to have a really nice transition into the announcements. It was hard to do but created a great audio/visual effect.

Universal Studios

This was another thing that I saw and instantly thought of it being an intro. I worked on it over winter break and got a pretty good result over just that week. I also made this in Cinema4D and the text can't have any of the effects that I needed for the project without purchasing the full version. I needed a much bigger stroke around the text, but it just wasn't possible. It looks alright as it is but still needed more lighting and a better globe model.

Mind Field

I worked on this one around the time of covid starting after seeing it as its original version and finished the basic version in a night, but I put it on the side until I needed to use it as a quick intro just to start the year.

The Twilight Zone

We once again needed an intro for the Halloween spooky season and decided on this one. Our advisor recorded the voice over and I got all the footage. The text breaking animation was pretty fun to do, but the stars were annoying. I cut out all the footage which actually turned out pretty well against the black background. I wanted to make the door thicker, but After Effects doesn't support extruding video layers, so I just had to stack 50 doors on top of each other which created a weird effect but it looks alright. The motion of some things look weird, but it's as close to the original as I could get. Overall, I'm pretty happy with it.

Winter 2020

This intro was just done on a whim over a few days because I didn't really have an idea or anything to use for a winter intro. The idea first started with the snow covering the screen, but that would have been too hard to implement, so I just settled for making it like drawing on a frosted window (like you would do on a school bus). The snow fall was done using a simple effect in After Effects and I think it turned out pretty well. The actual announcements that this intro'ed had a nice overlay of snow going throughout as well as the Now Playing graphics beforehand. I originally had the graphics for the lower third transition using the snow becoming super dense across the entire screen, but it turned out to destroy the bitrate of the stream, so I switched to it just covering a small section.

Criminal Minds

This intro is the one that I am the most proud of overall. I spent the summer planning to use it as the first intro, but it really didn't work out as that at all. I just used that summer as making the template where I would replace the footage later. It took until around November for me to start emailing teachers during my downtime of not working on other projects. When I did have a project that I needed to work on, I would get caught working on this one because it was just so much fun. I sent out about 100 emails to or from teachers to plan out times to come during their classes to record. Only one of them was staged and the rest had actual students in the classrooms. The footage was also very useful to use in other projects because it was a bulk selection of strong B-Roll of students in classes. I managed to get the last clips I needed the day before I planned to start running the intro and finished it over that weekend.

Throwback Month

After Wilson's Minds I had no other real ideas for intros, so I used this month to come up with more ideas. This month consisted of using two old intros per week and using the actual announcements set with the announcers (still virtually recording) placed in monitors sitting on the set. I even re-edited the wii startup to have more recent intros that people will be more likely to recognize. It turned out very well for the effort I put in.

Elevator Intro

This was my last idea for an intro, having an elevator open up to a random scene and different music every time and finally open up to the actual announcer. I even added in the sound of me spamming the button like "oh no oh no, not here not here" which I think is funny. It's definitely people's least favorite intro of the year, but hey, the competition is high.

Pawsitive News Segments:

Pawsitive news was a reoccurring video published to the school's facebook page used as a way to give or highlight things to the community.

Tech Tips

This was a segment started the year before that I continued into this year. It is a basis for giving some quick technology info to the people of the community. The audio throughout all of these is a bit inconsistent, which is a bit frustrating, but they are all fine on their own.

Meet Audio

This was the first Tech Tip of this virtual year. The technology department was getting a lot of calls from people because Google Meet was new to everyone, and audio settings was one of the most popular points. I recorded the google meet window with my camera on which I think has a nice touch. I even stealthily zoomed in on each of the windows in post so they would be a bit easier to read.

Cleaning Devices

This one was my idea because I had started to work on Chromebooks that were not very clean, so I thought it would be a good idea to give information on how to properly clean them (and also clarify that yes, it is good to clean electronics because some people think it will harm the device). This is probably my least favorite Tech Tip because the camera looks quite bad and there is no strong lighting.

Chromebook Tips

Using the feedback from the last time, this one has better lighting… but with the better lighting comes shadows. I had to do a minor chroma key on just the shadows to make them darker so they wouldn't stick out as much. I think the multiple angles also turned out really well.

Skyward

Skyward is the grading and student management application that our school used. A lot of students don't have the simple toggles enabled which make it so much easier to use, or they don't know how to get their test results.

Streaming

This is definitely my favorite of the tips. I put a lot of effort into each of the steps with the on screen instructions which made it very pleasing to watch. The animation with the X1 search bar expanding out to the screen makes me want to watch it over and over again.

Restarting Devices

This was definitely the most rushed one out of them because I was also working on the Esports spotlight that came later. We didn't have time to put anything on screen and my mask makes my voice muffled so it's pretty hard to watch.

Community Spotlight

Another segment of Pawsitive News was the Community Spotlight which showcased something interesting going on in the school. We rotated through and shared the job of editing these.

Homecoming

The homecoming spotlight was fun to make because I was able to go out on the field during the reveals to get footage. This really helped with my comfortability with these things because I was so nervous to do it the first time.

Dumpling Making

The East Asian Club came into school on one of the virtual days during this COVID year and made dumplings for all the staff. It was a nice experience, but the camera had its ND filter on so all of the footage turned out very dark which was annoying.

Esports

The Esports spotlight was the one that I put the most effort into and I was the most proud of. I was a co-founder of the Esports team so I was obviously passionate about it. I wrote the script, recorded footage, and edited the entire thing, which felt really good.

Promos/etc

These are promotions for the club or other organizations that we made throughout the year along with some other projects that we helped other students out with

Media Promo 2019

We were running low on members so we decided to recreate one of the old promos for media with new footage and a new voiceover. I think it turned out quite well and we got some new members from it.

Shrek Promo

The GSA was having a showing of Shrek and we realized the day before that we hadn't actually advertised it. We recorded something that day and I edited it into the night.

Media Promo 2020

We were once again low on involvement so we thought that the way to solve it would be to rebrand the club, but it actually wasn't. We got no new members and realized that it was probably just because of the COVID year. The video turned out well, though, and was fun to record because I had to memorize my path and script. There's some minor things that could be improved but we gave up after like 3 hours of recording and used the best take.

Smash Club Promo

After school closed and ended due to COVID, no new president was elected, so I took over the responsibility. I decided to make a fun promo that worked to show people where the room was, which is something that I had trouble with when I was new. I used the templates that I had made for the Smash Bros. Intro to help out with this one. I also had to deliver the script from memory which was annoying, but it wasn't too bad.

Art Classes Promo

One of the art teachers reached out to me asking for me to make a promo for their classes. I got footage in their classes and interviewed some of the seniors which turned out really well.

IrriUp

The group submitted their project to the Pennsylvania's Governor's STEM Challenge where they created a project and submitted it. They needed a video edited for it, so they reached out to Media. Our advisor recorded the footage of their interviews and they had some footage of their project that they recorded themselves. They came into the studio at the end of the school day and we were there until around 11 getting everything edited and we had a finished project by the end of the night. There were some small issues that I had to fix, but overall it was fine. (they also waited until two days before it was due). I do not think it won, but I could be wrong. Their project was an automatic/manual plant waterer on a wall and it wasn't very good, so they really didn't deserve to win, but I think the video turned out well.

Superintendent Address

At the end of the school years, the superintendent usually records a video summarizing and/or giving some more information about the coming year. I was in charge of editing it and it was a little bit difficult because he paused for way too long between his sentences. I was able to use the B roll that I got throughout the year so it turned out really well.

Older/Personal Projects

I started editing videos in 7th grade with my friend. We had fun making the bad things that we made and we eventually both got better. We started doing our own things and haven't talked in a while.

Going for a Walk

I recorded a walk I went on with my dad and edited it into a video for him for Father's day. There's a very minor mistake in the first 10 seconds where the audio cuts, but it was pretty good for a very small project.

Fried Rice

For a project in our Chinese class we had to record a video of us cooking some type of Chinese food and editing it together and bringing it into class. It turned out pretty well, but there's some things that I would do well.

Chinese Music Video

Another Chinese class project was recreating a music video of a Chinese song and making a presentation about the artist and its impact on culture and stuff. I was, obviously, in charge of making the video. I was quite limited with my tools because I didn't have a very sophisticated video editor, but it turned out very well. It's quite cheesy but still entertaining.

Utah/Colorado Video

My family and I went to Utah and Colorado and I had the idea to make a fun recap video. I was very annoying to my family with recording every little thing, but they really ate their words when they saw the finished video and were very impressed.
